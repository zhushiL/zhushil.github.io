About Me

I am a third-year master's student in Control Science and Engineering at Northeastern University. I will soon join Shanghai Jiao Tong University as a research assistant, advised by Prof. Daolin Ma.

My research focuses on 3D vision and embodied intelligence, especially scalable 3D perception, scene completion, and robot manipulation.

Recent focus: I am working on World Action Model research for embodied algorithms, with an emphasis on perception-conditioned robot manipulation.

PatchScene: Patch-based Voxel Diffusion for Large-Scale Scene Completion

CVPR 2026 Co-first Author
Qingdong Xu*, Jiajun Zhu*, Shilin Zhu*, Xinjing He, Chao Lu, Huanran Wang, Jiyao Zhang
Paper Demo

A diffusion framework for large-scale LiDAR scene completion. PatchScene generates fine-grained geometry in local voxel patches, fuses spatial and temporal context, and expands completion through an annular-flow strategy for scalable outdoor reconstruction.

VAP-SAM: Prompt-Free SAM for Insubstantial Gas Segmentation

IEEE TIM 2026 JCR Q1
Shilin Zhu, Qi Wang, Yixuan Jing, Mengge Yang
Paper Demo

Builds an open-source gas segmentation dataset and replaces manual prompt engineering with a visual autonomous perception module. The method distills prompt-trained SAM priors into a prompt-free segmentation pipeline.

IEEE Sensors Journal 2025 JCR Q1
Qi Wang, Shilin Zhu, Zhenlin Lu, Yunlong Sun, Yizhuo Qian
Paper

An efficient infrared gas leak detector that reduces redundant feature extraction, strengthens sensitivity to gas target spatial cues, and improves multi-scale feature fusion for small and diffuse gas regions.
